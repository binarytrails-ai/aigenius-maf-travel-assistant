# Australia & New Zealand Travel Destination Guide

Comprehensive guide to popular travel destinations in Australia and New Zealand with detailed information to help travelers make informed decisions.

## Australia - New South Wales

### Sydney

**Best for**: Beach lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, iconic landmarks, active travelers, urban explorers
**Best time to visit**: September-November, March-May (spring and autumn)
**Typical duration**: 4-7 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range to expensive
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- Iconic Opera House and Harbour Bridge
- Beautiful harbor and world-famous beaches (Bondi, Manly, Coogee)
- Excellent weather most of the year
- Outdoor lifestyle and water activities
- Coastal walks (Bondi to Coogee, Manly to Spit)
- Vibrant dining scene with multicultural influences
- Day trips to Blue Mountains, Hunter Valley wine region

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $100-400 AUD/night
- Meals: $50-150 AUD/day
- Transport: $15-30 AUD/day (Opal card for public transport)

**Considerations**:
- Expensive city (one of Australia's priciest)
- Summer (December-February) can be extremely hot (35°C+)
- Beaches crowded in summer and on weekends
- Traffic congestion during peak hours

---

### Blue Mountains

**Best for**: Nature lovers, hikers, photographers, day-trippers from Sydney
**Best time to visit**: March-May, September-November (autumn and spring)
**Typical duration**: 1-3 days
**Budget level**: Budget to mid-range
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- Dramatic cliff formations and eucalyptus forests
- Three Sisters rock formation at Katoomba
- Scenic walks and hiking trails (from easy to challenging)
- Waterfalls and lookout points
- Charming mountain villages (Leura, Katoomba)
- Jenolan Caves nearby
- Only 90 minutes from Sydney

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $80-250 AUD/night
- Meals: $30-80 AUD/day
- Transport: Train from Sydney $10-20 AUD return, or car rental

**Considerations**:
- Can be cold in winter (June-August), occasional snow
- Weekends very busy with Sydney day-trippers
- Some walks closed after heavy rain or fire risk
- Limited public transport within the mountains (car recommended)

---

### Byron Bay

**Best for**: Beach lovers, surfers, wellness seekers, alternative culture enthusiasts
**Best time to visit**: Year-round (subtropical climate), best September-May
**Typical duration**: 3-5 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- Beautiful beaches and excellent surf breaks
- Laid-back bohemian atmosphere
- Lighthouse walk with whale watching (June-October)
- Yoga retreats and wellness centers
- Vibrant farmers markets and organic food scene
- Hinterland waterfalls and rainforest walks nearby
- Most easterly point of mainland Australia

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $100-300 AUD/night
- Meals: $40-100 AUD/day
- Transport: $10-20 AUD/day (bike rental popular)

**Considerations**:
- Very popular and can be crowded
- Accommodation expensive during school holidays
- Limited parking in town center
- Winter (June-August) cooler for swimming but still pleasant

---

## Australia - Victoria

### Melbourne

**Best for**: Coffee lovers, food and wine enthusiasts, sports fans, culture and arts lovers, urban explorers
**Best time to visit**: September-May (spring through autumn)
**Typical duration**: 3-5 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range to expensive
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- World-class coffee culture and dining scene
- Street art and laneways
- Sports capital (Australian Open, F1, Melbourne Cup, AFL)
- Cultural experiences (NGV, Arts Centre, museums)
- Shopping from boutiques to markets (Queen Victoria Market)
- Diverse neighborhoods with distinct vibes (Fitzroy, St Kilda, South Yarra)
- Day trips to Great Ocean Road, Yarra Valley wineries, Phillip Island
- Excellent public transportation (trams, trains, buses)

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $100-350 AUD/night
- Meals: $50-120 AUD/day
- Transport: $10-20 AUD/day (free tram zone in CBD)

**Considerations**:
- Weather unpredictable ("four seasons in one day")
- Winter (June-August) can be cold and rainy
- Less beachy than Sydney (though St Kilda Beach available)
- Some areas less safe late at night

---

### Great Ocean Road

**Best for**: Scenic drives, photographers, nature lovers, coastal walk enthusiasts
**Best time to visit**: October-April (warmer weather), but year-round destination
**Typical duration**: 2-4 days (3 days ideal for full experience)
**Budget level**: Mid-range
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- Twelve Apostles limestone rock formations
- Dramatic coastal cliffs and beaches
- Loch Ard Gorge and other natural formations
- Great Otway National Park with rainforest walks
- Charming coastal towns (Lorne, Apollo Bay, Port Fairy)
- Wildlife spotting (koalas, kangaroos at Tower Hill)
- Surfing beaches (Bells Beach - famous surf spot)
- One of the world's most scenic coastal drives

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $80-250 AUD/night
- Meals: $40-100 AUD/day
- Transport: Car rental essential ($60-120 AUD/day)

**Considerations**:
- Car essential (no public transport along route)
- Can be cold and windy, even in summer
- Some sections closed occasionally due to landslides
- Gets very busy during summer holidays and Easter

---

### Grampians National Park

**Best for**: Hikers, rock climbers, wildlife enthusiasts, Indigenous culture seekers
**Best time to visit**: September-May (spring through autumn), wildflowers in spring
**Typical duration**: 2-3 days
**Budget level**: Budget to mid-range
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- Dramatic mountain ranges and rock formations
- Numerous hiking trails (from easy walks to challenging climbs)
- MacKenzie Falls and other waterfalls
- Ancient Indigenous rock art sites
- Abundant wildlife (kangaroos, emus, echidnas)
- Panoramic lookouts (Boroka Lookout, The Pinnacle)
- Rock climbing opportunities

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $70-200 AUD/night (Halls Gap base)
- Meals: $30-80 AUD/day
- Transport: Car rental recommended

**Considerations**:
- 3 hours drive from Melbourne
- Limited mobile phone coverage in some areas
- Summer can be very hot (bushfire risk)
- Book accommodation ahead in peak season

---

## Australia - Queensland

### Great Barrier Reef (Cairns & Port Douglas)

**Best for**: Divers, snorkelers, marine life enthusiasts, tropical paradise seekers
**Best time to visit**: April-November (dry season, best visibility)
**Typical duration**: 4-7 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range to expensive
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- World's largest coral reef system (UNESCO World Heritage)
- Incredible diving and snorkeling experiences
- Vibrant coral gardens and tropical fish
- Marine life encounters (turtles, reef sharks, manta rays)
- Tropical rainforest meets reef
- Cairns: Gateway to reef with lively backpacker scene
- Port Douglas: Upmarket reef access with relaxed vibe
- Day trips and liveaboard options
- Daintree Rainforest nearby (oldest rainforest in world)

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $80-350 AUD/night (Cairns cheaper, Port Douglas pricier)
- Meals: $40-100 AUD/day
- Reef tours: $150-300 AUD/day
- Transport: $10-20 AUD/day in town

**Considerations**:
- Wet season (December-March) brings rain, humidity, and jellyfish (stinger suits required)
- Best underwater visibility April-November
- Coral bleaching concerns (some areas recovering)
- Book reef tours ahead in peak season
- Hot and humid year-round

---

### Whitsunday Islands

**Best for**: Beach lovers, sailors, romantic getaways, island hopping enthusiasts
**Best time to visit**: April-October (dry season), avoid jellyfish season December-March
**Typical duration**: 3-5 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range to expensive
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- 74 tropical islands in the Great Barrier Reef
- Whitehaven Beach (voted world's best beach, pure silica sand)
- Sailing and yacht charters
- Snorkeling and diving at reef sites
- Hamilton Island resort base
- Heart Reef scenic flights
- Relaxed island atmosphere

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $150-500+ AUD/night (island resorts expensive)
- Meals: $50-120 AUD/day
- Sailing tours: $150-400 AUD/day
- Flights to Hamilton Island or ferry from Airlie Beach

**Considerations**:
- Expensive destination (limited competition on islands)
- Cyclone season November-April
- Jellyfish present in summer (wear stinger suits)
- Book sailing tours well ahead
- Ferry to islands can be choppy

---

### Gold Coast

**Best for**: Beach lovers, families, theme parks, surfers, partygoers
**Best time to visit**: Year-round (subtropical), peak December-February
**Typical duration**: 3-5 days
**Budget level**: Budget to mid-range
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- 70km of beaches and surf breaks
- Theme parks (Dreamworld, Movie World, Sea World)
- Vibrant nightlife at Surfers Paradise
- Hinterland rainforest and waterfalls nearby
- Canal and waterfront dining
- Surfing culture
- Family-friendly attractions

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $80-300 AUD/night
- Meals: $40-100 AUD/day
- Theme park tickets: $80-120 AUD/day (multi-day passes better value)
- Transport: $10-20 AUD/day (light rail along coast)

**Considerations**:
- Very touristy and commercial
- School holidays extremely busy
- Can be party-heavy (18-30s crowd)
- Summer very hot and humid
- Cyclones possible (rare but can occur)

---

### Sunshine Coast

**Best for**: Relaxed beach holidays, families, food lovers, nature enthusiasts
**Best time to visit**: Year-round (subtropical), best April-November
**Typical duration**: 3-7 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- More relaxed alternative to Gold Coast
- Beautiful beaches (Noosa, Mooloolaba, Coolum)
- Noosa National Park coastal walks
- Eumundi Markets (famous craft market)
- Australia Zoo (Steve Irwin's zoo nearby)
- Glass House Mountains for hiking
- Excellent food and cafe scene

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $100-300 AUD/night
- Meals: $40-100 AUD/day
- Transport: Car rental recommended ($50-100 AUD/day)

**Considerations**:
- Less nightlife than Gold Coast
- Car essential to explore properly
- Accommodation expensive in Noosa during peak
- Summer hot and humid

## Australia - South Australia

### Adelaide & Barossa Valley

**Best for**: Wine lovers, food enthusiasts, cultural experiences, relaxed city breaks
**Best time to visit**: March-May, September-November (mild weather, harvest season)
**Typical duration**: 3-5 days (2 days city, 1-2 days wine region)
**Budget level**: Mid-range
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- Vibrant arts and festival scene
- Adelaide Central Market (food paradise)
- Barossa Valley wine region (45min drive, world-class wineries)
- Beautiful parklands surrounding  CBD
- Beach suburbs (Glenelg)
- McLaren Vale wine region also nearby
- Affordable compared to Sydney/Melbourne

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $90-250 AUD/night
- Meals: $40-100 AUD/day
- Wine tours: $100-200 AUD/day
- Transport: $10-15 AUD/day (free city tram loop)

**Considerations**:
- Smaller city, fewer attractions than Sydney/Melbourne
- Summer (December-February) very hot (40°C+)
- Limited  late-night public transport
- Wine region requires car or tour

## Australia - Western Australia

### Perth & Margaret River

**Best for**: Beach lovers, wine enthusiasts, isolated beauty seekers, relaxed pace
**Best time to visit**: September-May (warm and dry), avoid winter June-August
**Typical duration**: 4-7 days (3 days Perth, 2-3 days Margaret River)
**Budget level**: Mid-range
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- Beautiful uncrowded beaches (Cottesloe, Scarborough)
- Margaret River wine region (3-hour drive south)
- World-class surfing breaks
- Rottnest Island day trip (quokkas, cycling, beaches)
- Kings Park botanical gardens
- One of the world's most isolated cities
- Relaxed, sunny lifestyle

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $100-300 AUD/night
- Meals: $40-100 AUD/day
- Transport: $10-20 AUD/day (car rental for Margaret River)

**Considerations**:
- Very isolated (4-hour flight from east coast)
- Limited international flights
- Margaret River wine region needs car
- Summer very hot
- Expensive compared to east coast

## Australia - Northern Territory

### Uluru (Ayers Rock)

**Best for**: Cultural experiences, photographers, bucket-list travelers, nature lovers
**Best time to visit**: April-October (cooler weather), avoid summer December-February
**Typical duration**: 2-4 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range to expensive
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- Iconic red rock monolith and cultural significance
- Sunrise and sunset experiences
- Indigenous culture and art experiences
- Field of Light art installation
- Kata Tjuta (The Olgas) nearby
- Star gazing in outback
- Base walks and cultural tours

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $150-400+ AUD/night (limited options)
- Meals: $50-120 AUD/day
- Park entry: $38 AUD (3-day pass)
- Flights to Ayers Rock Airport expensive

**Considerations**:
- Expensive and remote destination
- Summer extremely hot (45°C+)
- Limited accommodation options (book ahead)
- Flies can be annoying (bring fly net)
- Culturally sensitive site (climbing prohibited)

---

### Kakadu National Park

**Best for**: Nature lovers, wildlife enthusiasts, Indigenous culture seekers, adventurers
**Best time to visit**: April-October (dry season), wet season inaccessible
**Typical duration**: 3-5 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Ancient Indigenous rock art
- Dramatic waterfalls (Jim Jim Falls, Twin Falls)
- Abundant wildlife (crocodiles, birds, wallabies)
- Cruises on Yellow Water Billabong
- Remote outback landscape
- 4WD adventures

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $100-250 AUD/night (limited options)
- Meals: $40-80 AUD/day
- Park entry: $40 AUD (7-day pass)
- Tours: $100-200 AUD/day

**Considerations**:
- 3-hour drive from Darwin
- Wet season (November-March) many areas closed/flooded
- Very hot and humid
- Saltwater crocodiles (only swim in designated areas)
- Remote (bring supplies)
- 4WD recommended for best access

---

## Australia - Tasmania

### Hobart & Tasmania

**Best for**: Nature lovers, foodies, wilderness seekers, whisky enthusiasts, cool-climate travelers
**Best time to visit**: October-April (warmer months), December-February peak
**Typical duration**: 5-10 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range
**Language**: English

**Highlights**:
- Pristine wilderness and national parks
- Cradle Mountain-Lake St Clair National Park
- Freycinet Peninsula and Wineglass Bay
- MONA (Museum of Old and New Art) - world-class museum
- Historic Port Arthur penal settlement
- Salamanca Market (Saturdays in Hobart)
- Fresh produce, seafood, wines, and whisky
- Bay of Fires on east coast
- Bruny Island day trip

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $90-300 AUD/night
- Meals: $40-100 AUD/day
- Transport: Car rental essential ($50-100 AUD/day)
- National park passes

**Considerations**:
- Car absolutely essential (distances large, limited public transport)
- Weather changeable (bring layers)
- Winter (June-August) cold and rainy
- Some mountain roads closed in winter
- Ferry from Melbourne (overnight) or fly
- Book accommodation ahead (limited in remote areas)

## New Zealand - North Island

### Auckland

**Best time to visit**: September-May (spring through autumn), December-February warmest
**Typical duration**: 2-4 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range
**Language**: English, Te Reo Māori

**Highlights**:
- Sky Tower and waterfront precincts
- Harbor islands (Waiheke Island wine day trip, Rangitoto)
- Volcanic landscape (One Tree Hill, Mt Eden)
- Diverse neighborhoods and food scene
- Black sand beaches on west coast
- Gateway to Bay of Islands and Hobbiton
- Cosmopolitan dining and nightlife

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $100-350 NZD/night
- Meals: $40-100 NZD/day
- Transport: $10-20 NZD/day

**Considerations**:
- Expensive city
- Weather can be rainy
- Traffic congestion
- Less "scenic NZ" than South Island

**Best for**: Cultural experiences, geothermal wonders, adventure activities, Māori culture
**Best time to visit**: October-April (warmer weather)
**Typical duration**: 2-3 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range
**Language**: English, Te Reo Māori

**Highlights**:
- Geothermal parks with geysers and hot pools
- Māori cultural experiences (hangi, performances)
- Adventure activities (zorbing, luging, skydiving)

- Hot pools and spa experiences

- Meals: $35-80 NZD/day
- Geothermal park entry: $30-80 NZD
- Cultural experiences: $80-150 NZD

**Considerations**:
- Strong sulfur smell in town
- Very touristy

- Can be combined with Hobbiton day trip

**Budget level**: Mid-range

- Beautiful beaches and clear waters
- Dolphin watching and swimming with dolphins

## New Zealand - South Island

### Queenstown

**Best for**: Adventure seekers, scenic beauty lovers, wine enthusiasts, all-season destination
**Best time to visit**: Year-round (summer for hiking, winter for skiing)
**Typical duration**: 4-7 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range to expensive
**Language**: English, Te Reo Māori

**Highlights**:
- Adventure capital of the world (bungee jumping, skydiving, jet boating)
- Stunning alpine and lake scenery
- World-class skiing (June-October)
- Milford Sound day trips
- Central Otago wineries
- Hiking trails (Routeburn, Ben Lomond)
- Vibrant dining and nightlife scene
- Scenic Glenorchy and Paradise Valley

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $120-400+ NZD/night
- Meals: $50-120 NZD/day
- Activities: $100-400+ NZD each
- Transport: $10-20 NZD/day in town

**Considerations**:
- Expensive destination
- Very busy in peak seasons
- Winter skiing season June-October
- Book activities and accommodation ahead
- Weather changeable

---

### Milford Sound

**Best for**: Nature lovers, photographers, scenic cruises, hikers (Milford Track)
**Best time to visit**: October-April (summer), but rain common year-round
**Typical duration**: 1-2 days (day trip from Queenstown or Te Anau)
**Budget level**: Mid-range to expensive
**Language**: English, Te Reo Māori

**Highlights**:
- Dramatic fiord with waterfalls and rainforest
- UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Cruise options (2-hour scenic cruises)
- Mitre Peak and Bowen Falls
- Seal colonies and dolphins
- Milford Track (one of NZ's Great Walks - multi-day hike)
- "Eighth wonder of the world"

**Cost indicators**:
- Day tour from Queenstown: $200-300 NZD
- Cruise: $70-150 NZD
- Scenic flight add-on: $200-400 NZD

**Considerations**:
- 4-hour drive from Queenstown (long day trip)
- Often rains (250+ days/year) but beautiful in rain
- Book ahead in summer
- Road can close in winter
- Consider scenic flight option

---

**Typical duration**: 2-4 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range

### Abel Tasman National Park

**Best for**: Kayakers, beach lovers, trampers (hikers), nature enthusiasts
**Best time to visit**: December-March (warmest), but year-round destination
**Typical duration**: 2-4 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range
**Language**: English, Te Reo Māori

**Highlights**:
- Golden beaches and turquoise waters
- Coastal walking track (one of NZ's Great Walks)
- Sea kayaking opportunities
- Water taxis to remote beaches
- Seals and birdlife
- New Zealand's smallest and most picturesque national park

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $80-250 NZD/night (Nelson base or park lodges)
- Meals: $35-80 NZD/day
- Kayak rentals: $70-150 NZD/day
- Water taxis: $40-80 NZD

**Considerations**:
- Book huts/campsites ahead (Great Walk)
- Water taxis convenient for sections
- Flies and sandflies can be annoying
- Best in summer

---

### Franz Josef & Fox Glaciers

**Best for**: Glacier enthusiasts, hikers, scenic beauty seekers
**Best time to visit**: October-April, but accessible year-round
**Typical duration**: 2-3 days
**Budget level**: Mid-range to expensive
**Language**: English, Te Reo Māori

**Highlights**:
- Two glaciers descending into rainforest
- Glacier valley walks
- Heli-hikes and scenic flights
- Hot pools at Franz Josef
- Rainforest and mountain scenery
- Unique rainforest-glacier ecosystem

**Cost indicators**:
- Accommodation: $100-300 NZD/night
- Meals: $40-90 NZD/day
- Heli-hike: $400-500 NZD
- Scenic flights: $200-350 NZD

**Considerations**:
- Weather very changeable (high rainfall)
- Heli-hikes weather-dependent (book but may cancel)
- Glaciers receding due to climate change
- Small towns with limited services
- Book ahead in summer

## Recommendation Matrices

### Best Destinations by Traveler Type

**First-Time Australia/NZ Travelers**: Sydney, Melbourne, Queenstown, Rotorua
**Budget Travelers**: Gold Coast, Adelaide, Hobart, Christchurch
**Luxury Travelers**: Whitsundays, Port Douglas, Queenstown, Bay of Islands
**Solo Travelers**: Melbourne, Sydney, Queenstown, Auckland (safe, social scenes)
**Couples/Romance**: Whitsundays, Byron Bay, Queenstown, Bay of Islands
**Families**: Gold Coast, Sunshine Coast, Sydney, Rotorua
**Food & Wine Lovers**: Melbourne, Adelaide/Barossa, Hobart, Queenstown
**Beach Lovers**: Whitsundays, Byron Bay, Gold Coast, Sunshine Coast, Bay of Islands
**Nature/Wildlife**: Great Barrier Reef, Tasmania, Kakadu, Abel Tasman, Milford Sound
**Adventure Seekers**: Queenstown, Cairns, Rotorua, Tasmania
**Cultural Experiences**: Uluru, Rotorua, Sydney, Melbourne, Hobart (MONA)

### Best Destinations by Budget

**Budget ($50-100 AUD/NZD per day)**:
- Gold Coast (beach accommodation, self-catering)
- Adelaide (affordable city, cheap wine region tours)
- Sunshine Coast (outside Noosa)
- Christchurch (budget-friendly base)

**Mid-range ($100-200 AUD/NZD per day)**:
- Sydney (mix hostels/mid-range hotels)
- Melbourne (great value dining)
- Cairns (backpacker infrastructure)
- Byron Bay (outside peak season)
- Auckland
- Rotorua
- Hobart
- Most regional areas

**Higher Budget ($200-400+ AUD/NZD per day)**:
- Whitsundays (island resorts expensive)
- Uluru (remote, limited options)
- Port Douglas (upmarket reef access)
- Queenstown (adventure activities add up)
- Milford Sound (tours expensive)
- Tasmania lodges

### Best Destinations by Season

**December-February (Summer)**:
- **Ideal**: Tasmania, Melbourne, Adelaide, South Island NZ, Queenstown
- **Hot but popular**: Sydney, Gold Coast, Byron Bay, Perth
- **Avoid**: Far North Queensland (wet season, jellyfish), Kakadu (wet/closed)

**March-May (Autumn)**:
- **Ideal**: Most of Australia and NZ (shoulder season - perfect weather)
- **Best value**: Whitsundays, Great Barrier Reef, Sydney, Melbourne
- **Highlights**: Barossa harvest season (wine), autumn colors in Tasmania/NZ

**June-August (Winter)**:
- **Ideal**: Far North Queensland (dry season), Whitsundays, Kakadu
- **Ski season**: Queenstown NZ, some Australian alps
- **Avoid**: Tasmania (cold), Melbourne (cold/wet), southern NZ (very cold)

**September-November (Spring)**:
- **Ideal**: Nearly everywhere (best time overall)
- **Highlights**: Wildflowers in WA, warming up in Tasmania
- **Best value**: Shoulder season pricing

## Multi-Destination Trip Ideas

**Classic East Coast Australia (1-2 weeks)**:
Sydney (3 days) → Byron Bay (2 days) → Gold Coast (2 days) → Brisbane (1 day) → Whitsundays (3 days) → Cairns/Great Barrier Reef (3 days)

**Victoria & Tasmania (10-14 days)**:
Melbourne (3 days) → Great Ocean Road (2 days) → Grampians (2 days) → Ferry to Tasmania → Hobart (2 days) → Freycinet (2 days) → Cradle Mountain (2 days)

**South Island NZ Loop (10-14 days)**:
Christchurch (2 days) → Queenstown (4 days, with Milford Sound day trip) → Franz Josef (2 days) → Abel Tasman (3 days) → back to Christchurch

**Australia Highlights (3 weeks)**:
Sydney (4 days) → Melbourne (3 days) → Great Ocean Road (2 days) → Adelaide/Barossa (3 days) → Uluru (3 days) → Cairns/Great Barrier Reef (4 days) → Whitsundays (3 days)

**New Zealand Grand Tour (2-3 weeks)**:
Auckland (2 days) → Bay of Islands (3 days) → Rotorua (2 days) → Fly to Christchurch → Queenstown (4 days) → Milford Sound (1 day) → Franz Josef (2 days) → Abel Tasman (3 days) → back to Auckland

## General Australia & New Zealand Travel Wisdom

### Hidden Costs to Consider
- Domestic flights (distances large in Australia)
- Car rentals and fuel (often essential)
- National park entry fees
- Activity costs (NZ adventures expensive)
- Alcohol prices (expensive in both countries)
- Reef tours and boat trips

### Currency
- Australia: Australian Dollar (AUD)
- New Zealand: New Zealand Dollar (NZD)
- Both roughly similar value (slightly different exchange rates)

### Making Destinations Work on Different Budgets

**Budget Version**: Hostels/budget accommodation, self-catering, public transport where possible, free activities (beaches, walks), pack lunches

**Mid-range**: 3-4 star hotels/motels, mix of dining out and self-catering, car rental for flexibility, main attractions and some paid activities

**Luxury**: 4-5 star hotels/lodges, fine dining, private tours and transfers, all activities, helicopter flights, wine tastings
